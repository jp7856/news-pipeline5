NE Times Junior measured level spec (from analysis of published articles):

| Sub-level | Avg words | Avg sentence length (words) | CEFR |
|---|---|---|---|
| L1 | 111 | 11.6 | A2+ |
| L2 | 171 | 13.7 | early B1 |
| L3 | 199 | 12.4 | B1 |

Write at the L2 level by default:
- About 155–190 words total, in 4–5 paragraphs.
- Average sentence length about 13–14 words; mix short and medium sentences.
- Compound and simple complex sentences are fine (because/when/if/that-clauses,
  basic relative clauses). Avoid stacking more than one subordinate clause.
- Tense variety is expected (present, past, present perfect); simple passives okay.
- Standard news-article register. Connectives (however, for example, as a result)
  should appear naturally — they are a key difficulty marker at this level.
- Vocabulary: lower-secondary textbook level; topic-specific terms are allowed
  with a short gloss when first used.
- Note: this media overlaps with NE Times Kids L3 below and NE Times L1 above —
  aim squarely at the middle, not the edges.
