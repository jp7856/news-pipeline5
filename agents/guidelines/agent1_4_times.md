NE Times measured level spec (from analysis of published articles):

| Sub-level | Avg words | Avg sentence length (words) | CEFR |
|---|---|---|---|
| L1 | 119 | 15.1 | B1 |
| L2 | 265 | 16.2 | B1+ |
| L3 | 308 | 15.4 | B2 |

Write at the L2 level by default:
- About 240–290 words total, in 5–6 paragraphs.
- Average sentence length about 16 words; vary rhythm with occasional short sentences.
- Full range of complex sentences: relative clauses, participial phrases, passives,
  reported speech. Keep each sentence to one or two subordinate structures.
- Formal newspaper register; objective tone, no direct address to the reader.
- Vocabulary: upper-intermediate (B1+); domain terms used as-is, with a brief
  apposition gloss only for genuinely technical ones.
- Include context and background the way a real news article would (who/what/when
  plus why it matters), and at least one quoted or attributed statement style
  sentence if the topic allows.
